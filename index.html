The Space

Breath. Stillness.
Then something shifts.

You arrive. The door closes on the outside world. A warm drink, soft music, a room that asks nothing of you. Karen guides a gentle meditation. Your breath slows. Your shoulders drop.

Then the art materials appear. Not to make something beautiful — to let something true move through your hands. Pastels, paint, collage. No right way. No judgment. Just you, meeting yourself on the page.

By the end, something feels lighter. Not because anyone fixed anything — but because you finally had space to feel it, name it, and let it be.

The Approach

Body. Mind. Spirit.

Somatic Experiencing

Guided breath work and body awareness practices that help you notice what's held in the body — and gently release it.

Meditative Art

Process-oriented intuitive art — no skill required. The making itself becomes meditation, and what emerges on the page often surprises.

Mindfulness & Connection

Learn to observe thought patterns without being swept away. Small groups of up to 8 create a safe circle for sharing and growth.

Meet Karen

Karen Gilbert M.Ed (C&D)

The older I get, the more I love the person I am becoming.

Karen knows what it feels like to be stuck. Anxiety, depression, grief — she's walked through all of it. That lived experience, combined with decades of professional practice as a psychotherapist and counsellor, shapes everything about how she holds space.

Her approach weaves humour, education, and experiential learning together. Healing, she believes, doesn't have to be so serious and heavy. Through breath, somatic experiencing, and meditative art, she helps women reconnect with parts of themselves they'd forgotten were there.

Master of Education (Counselling & Development), University of Tasmania · Psychotherapist · Family Counsellor · Energy Healing Practitioner · Based in Hobart since her late 20s · Grandmother
